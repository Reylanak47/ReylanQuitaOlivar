Who I Am

I’m a student developer who spends a lot of time in front of a laptop—coding, learning, gaming, and turning random ideas into working projects. I like building things that feel useful in real life, not just something to submit for a requirement.

I’m especially interested in web development and backend logic. I enjoy taking a simple idea like “what if there was a better way to find a boarding house?” and slowly turning it into a real system like BalayKo.

Most of what I know comes from trial and error: reading docs, breaking things, fixing them, and slowly understanding how everything connects—from the front end to the database.

How I Learn & Work

I learn by doing. That means dealing with Git errors, fixing broken layouts, and trying different approaches until something finally works. I commit my progress to GitHub so I can see how my code and thinking improve over time.

I like starting with something simple, then improving it bit by bit—cleaning the UI, organizing the folders, refining features, and making the system more stable.

Outside of Code

When I’m not coding, I’m usually in front of the same laptop—but gaming, watching videos, or planning ideas I may or may not actually build. I like switching between “deep focus” mode and “chill mode” so I don’t burn out while learning.
